The difference between being early and simply being wrong

Patience, evidence and the dangerous comfort of giving every bad idea more time.

By @42999group 16 July 2026 5 min read
Side note 01

“The market will understand later” is a thesis only when you can explain what it does not understand now.

Being early and being wrong have almost identical user interfaces. Both involve waiting. Both involve explaining yourself to skeptical friends. Both can produce a folder full of screenshots labeled “for later.”

The difference usually becomes obvious only after the fact, which is not very helpful while you are living through it. So we invent stories. We call the discomfort conviction. We call the lack of confirmation a time horizon. Sometimes this is exactly right. Sometimes we are furnishing a very comfortable room inside a mistake.

Time is not evidence

A good idea may take years to become visible. That does not mean every idea becomes better by waiting. Time can reveal value, but it does not manufacture it.

When I think I am early, I now ask what should change while I wait. Are users arriving without incentives? Is the product getting easier to explain? Are builders choosing the system for reasons beyond the token? A thesis should have observable consequences before it has a satisfying outcome.

“Patience is useful when reality is slowly agreeing with you. It is expensive when reality keeps sending polite corrections.”

Conviction should have conditions

Internet conviction is often presented as a permanent personality trait. You believe, therefore you are strong. You update, therefore you lacked vision. This is excellent material for motivational posts and terrible material for making decisions.

A useful conviction has exit conditions. Not only a price, but a change in the underlying reason. If the users never arrive, if the product remains a demo, if the team stops shipping or if the original advantage disappears, the story has changed.

Personal system

Write the reason before opening a position. Reading your own sentence later is cheaper than rewriting history.

The social cost matters

Leaving a thesis is harder when the thesis became an identity. Public positions create invisible debt. The more confidently we announce something, the more embarrassing it feels to update. That embarrassment is often mispriced as evidence.

I like people who can say, “I still find this interesting, but I was wrong about the timing.” I like them even more when they can say, “I was wrong about the whole thing.” Intellectual independence is not the ability to hold forever. It is the ability to change without requiring a new tribe first.

Early is a temporary description

If a thesis is right, early eventually becomes timely. Usage appears. Language gets clearer. The strange thing becomes ordinary. If none of that happens, “early” turns into a permanent exemption from evaluation.

I still believe patience is an edge. I also believe patience needs a job. Its job is to give a real process enough time to compound, not to protect every opinion from contact with the present.
