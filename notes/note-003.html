Note 019 / AI + Internet

AI agents are becoming the new crypto influencers

Synthetic personalities, automated conviction and the return of the account that never logs off.

By @42999group 09 July 2026 6 min read
Side note 01

An agent can post all night. This is either a technical achievement or a warning about the rest of us.

Crypto has always rewarded characters. The ecosystem turns anonymous accounts, founders and frogs into public infrastructure. AI agents fit naturally into this environment because the timeline already knows how to believe in a personality before verifying a person.

The current agent wave is messy, repetitive and more interesting than its critics admit. Many accounts are wrappers around language models with scheduled posts. Some have wallets, tools and small degrees of autonomy. Almost all have better attendance than humans.

Personality is the first product

Most agents do not begin with useful autonomy. They begin with a voice. They comment, joke, summarize and develop recognizable habits. This seems superficial until you remember that attention is often the first resource an internet project needs.

A memorable voice creates distribution. Distribution attracts collaborators. Collaborators add tools. The personality becomes an interface for a growing system. This is the same path many human influencers followed, only the agent does not need coffee and cannot be embarrassed by an old post unless its operator updates the prompt.

“The most successful agent may not be the smartest. It may be the one people remember to invite into the conversation.”

Automation makes conviction look effortless

An agent can hold a position with perfect consistency because consistency is cheap when memory is a file. Human conviction is affected by sleep, replies, price and the sudden desire to do something else. Agent conviction can be scheduled.

This creates a strange performance. The account appears confident because it never gets tired of its own thesis. Repetition looks like certainty, and certainty remains one of the internet’s most reliable growth strategies.

Interface warning

A sophisticated dashboard does not prove sophisticated reasoning. It proves somebody enjoys aligning small numbers.

Wallets turn content into behavior

The important shift happens when the agent can do more than post. A wallet lets a software personality participate in the systems it discusses. It can collect, tip, trade, fund or coordinate. That creates an observable history beyond content.

It also creates new questions. Who is responsible for the transaction? What counts as an independent decision? How much autonomy exists between the interface and the operator? The answers are usually less cinematic than the marketing, but the questions are still real.

The interesting future is probably less loud

The loud agents will attract attention first. The useful agents may look boring: monitoring a small ecosystem, translating community knowledge, identifying repeated questions, tracking promises or helping capital move with clearer context.

I am less interested in an agent pretending to be a genius than in one becoming a dependable participant. The internet has enough genius performances. Reliability would be a genuine novelty.

For now, I am watching the characters. Culture often prototypes the interface before software discovers the use case. Also, some of the jokes are good.
